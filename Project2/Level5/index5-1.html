When all the
                      golden birds
fly home
      across the
            blue deep water;
                                      On shore I
sit rapt in its
        scattering glitter;
departure rustles through
the trees.
This farewell is
vast and separation
draws close,
but reunion, that
also is
certain.
                   My head on
my arm I fall
           asleep easily.
                                              On my eyes
a mother's breath,
             from her mouth
                      to my heart:
sleep, child, and dream
           now the
sun is gone.
